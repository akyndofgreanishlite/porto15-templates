2016

Localytics, Version 4

In late 2014 when I joined Localytics as a full-time employee, they were focusing on making mobile marketing their primary focus, adding three new channels in 2015 alone. I saw this as a huge opportunity to establish a standardized component library, and reinvision the dashboard product as something much more task-driven.

Building a foundation

Prototyping with Angular was already an integral part of the design process, but by evolving this approach into a library that designers and developers could co-own, we expedited user testing and the ability to quickly update components across the entire production dashboard.

A dashboard centric approach

Referencing sample dashboards from Localytics’ documentation, I began reorienting the web app around a small set of User Lifecycle themed dashboards—Acquire, Grow, Engage, & Retain.

Each Lifecycle dashboard helps customers answer three key questions about given theme:

  • What feature or campaign isn’t working?

  • How can I fix it?

  • Where other audiences or metrics can I look at to make improvements or drive engagement?

Action-oriented marketing & analytics

Planning a communication layer

To bring these Lifecycle segment dashboards to life, I began by collecting all of the tasks, metrics, and insights we could surface to customers through a communication layer. This content, when surfaced in the right way, could sum up the vast quantities of data being captured from app usage, in the places and times that customers needed them the most.

Surfacing actions and insights

We wanted users to have a stream of easily digestible of tasks as a jumping off point every time they visited the dashboard, so that they could spend their time tackling problematic metrics and campaigns, and also have easy access to insights surfaced programmatically, making the most of their time in the dashboard and growing with the user.

To make this a reality, we wanted to come to the users on their terms, regardless of whether a weekly digest email or a realtime mobile app was the most convenient channel for them.

Re-building the campaign creation process from the ground up

Another area with a major opportunity for improvement was the campaign building process, and to reduce enormous amount of complexity possible, we changed the process from a step-based flow to one where a summary of only required fields were initially shown to the user. We also moved complex tasks, like audience selection/creation, into modals, so that the user could focus on one major task at a time.
